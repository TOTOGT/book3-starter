Lessons & Assignments

Reading the world in two languages at once

Each lesson is a 120-minute B2–C1 unit pairing a piece of real news, a piece of real history, and a thread from the AXLE / Principia Orthogona framework. Students leave with vocabulary, grammar, and a story worth carrying home. Designed for adult learners at Hour House, 229 Ballantine Pkwy, Newark, NJ.

How these lessons work

Each lesson follows the CAELA Framework for Effective Lesson Planning for Adult English Language Learners: a seven-stage arc from warm-up to application, with explicit vocabulary, grammar focus, listening and reading inputs, and structured speaking and writing outputs. The companion side of each lesson links to the corresponding chapter in the Principia Orthogona series — so a learner who wants to go further into the science, mathematics, or history behind the lesson has a clear next door to walk through. Every lesson is free to read on this page; the deeper companion texts live in the membership at g6llc.gumroad.com.

Lesson 01 B2 · multi-lesson unit

The Vitruvian Approximation

From the Rhind Papyrus to Da Vinci — 3,500 years of measuring with the body

A multi-lesson unit on how human beings have used the body — and the shapes the body makes — to measure the world. Students enter through Leonardo's Vitruvian Man, the famous figure inscribed in a circle and a square, and discover that the same circle-and-square question was answered three thousand years earlier in the Rhind Mathematical Papyrus (Egypt, c. 1650 BCE), which gives the area of a circle as the square on 8/9 of its diameter. Vocabulary work on proportion and approximation; grammar on comparatives, superlatives, and the language of estimation ("about," "roughly," "nearly"). The companion paper — The Vitruvian Approximation (Nogueira Grossi, 2026) — proves the Rhind formula as one G-cycle of the C→K→F→U contact-mechanics operator chain: a rational circle of diameter d becomes a rational square of area (8d/9)², with the central statement carried in Lean 4.

Lesson 02 · NEW B2–C1 · 120 min

The Galilean Confluence

Galileo's margins in Ptolemy's Almagest — Florence, 2026

A current-events lesson built around the January 2026 discovery by historian Ivan Malara of Galileo's own handwritten notes in a 1551 copy of Ptolemy's Almagest. Students watch a 2-minute WTVG news segment, read a Smithsonian feature, build a 2,000-year timeline, and practise the past perfect — then write their own "marginalia" about an idea they once believed and have since outgrown.

The Saturn Hexagon

Geometry from a place that has no geometers

A lesson on the strange six-sided cloud pattern at Saturn's north pole — discovered by Voyager, confirmed by Cassini — and what it teaches us about how shape emerges in fluids. Vocabulary: vortex, latitude, rotation, anomaly. Grammar: passive voice for scientific description.
